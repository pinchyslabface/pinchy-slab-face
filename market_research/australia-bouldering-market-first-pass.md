# Australia Bouldering Market First Pass

## Executive Summary

- `Fact` The best clearly cited participation anchor I found for Australia-wide climbing is the 2019-20 AusPlay climbing release summarised by Sport Climbing WA: **218.8k participants** in the broad climbing category, including **205.2k adults** and **13.6k children**. Adults were reported as **36% female** and children **54% female**. Source: [Sport Climbing WA](https://www.sportclimbingwa.com.au/news/ausplay-2019-20-survey-data-released).
- `Fact` By July 2024, ABC reporting on newer AusPlay data said Australia had **100k+ sport climbing participants**, plus another **350k** involved in outdoor rock climbing, abseiling, or caving. Source: [ABC, 22 July 2024](https://www.abc.net.au/news/2024-07-22/sport-trends-changing-australia-climbing-hang-gliding-frisbee/104114052).
- `Inference` The indoor climbing audience is now large enough to support a city-first media product in Australia, but it is still concentrated in a handful of metros rather than evenly spread nationally.
- `Estimate` Australia appears to have **at least 70 confirmed commercial indoor climbing venues**, with a likely true count in the **70-80 range** once smaller regional operators and recent openings are fully reconciled. Confidence is medium because no single current national source is complete.
- `Fact` Mountain Project currently lists **40 Australian gyms**, which is useful as a seed list but clearly incomplete versus current operator, state-association, and venue websites. Source: [Mountain Project Australia gyms](https://www.mountainproject.com/gyms/australia).
- `Fact` Pricing signals are relatively consistent across the market: casual entry commonly sits around **A$21-A$25**, shoe hire around **A$5-A$7**, and recurring memberships often sit around **A$30-A$36 per week** depending on commitment length and venue bundle. Sources include [Urban Climb pricing](https://urbanclimb.com.au/pricing), [Climb Fit pricing](https://www.climbfit.com.au/pricing/), [Beyond Bouldering pricing](https://beyondbouldering.com.au/pricing/), and [CAWA gym discounts](https://climberswa.au/climb/gyms/).
- `Inference` Melbourne remains the strongest PSF launch market on this first pass because it combines dense venue supply, broad format mix, strong inner-city clustering, and enough scene complexity to produce recurring newsletter utility. Sydney is a close second; Brisbane is the clearest third.

### Interpretation

- `Inference` The biggest methodological trap is category drift. Older AusPlay reporting grouped climbing with rock climbing, abseiling, and caving, while newer reporting separates out sport climbing.
- `Inference` The cleanest planning assumption for PSF is:
  indoor / sport climbing audience in Australia is probably a **six-figure market**
  broader “climbing-adjacent” audience is significantly larger
- `Estimate` A practical working range for PSF is **100k-220k Australians with meaningful relevance to indoor climbing**, depending on whether you use the narrow sport-climbing lens or the broader climbing-participation lens.

## Demographics And Participation Signals

### Hard evidence

- `Fact` Sport Climbing WA’s AusPlay summary reports:
  adults: 205.2k
  kids: 13.6k
  adult female share: 36%
  child female share: 54%
  reasons for climbing: fun/enjoyment, physical health or fitness, psychological/mental health/therapy, and social reasons
  Source: [Sport Climbing WA](https://www.sportclimbingwa.com.au/news/ausplay-2019-20-survey-data-released)

### Strong directional signals

- `Fact` ABC’s July 2024 climbing story frames bouldering as social, cooperative, and more approachable than traditional gym culture, and links participation growth to the Tokyo 2021 Olympic debut and broader visibility from `Free Solo`. Source: [ABC](https://www.abc.net.au/news/2024-07-22/sport-trends-changing-australia-climbing-hang-gliding-frisbee/104114052)
- `Fact` Many current gyms prominently market beginner offers, youth programs, family passes, women-only or trans-inclusive sessions, and coaching:
  [Beyond Bouldering](https://beyondbouldering.com.au/)
  [Northside Boulders](https://northsideboulders.com/)
  [Climb Fit](https://www.climbfit.com.au/pricing/)
  [Gecko Climb Darwin](https://geckoclimb.com.au/)

### What this likely means

- `Inference` Indoor climbing in Australia is not just an advanced-climber niche anymore. The live market clearly includes:
  beginners and casual social climbers
  youth and family segments
  students and young professionals
  committed performance climbers
- `Inference` The gender split in children being more balanced than adults suggests a long-term opportunity: if gyms and media products retain more of that youth cohort into adulthood, the audience broadens over time.
- `Inference` The strongest recurring motivations for PSF are likely social utility plus scene identity, not only pure training or elite performance.

## Spend And Commercial Signals

### National participation spending

- `Fact` The Australian Sports Commission said Australians spent **A$16.4b** on sport and physical activity in the year to December 2022, including **A$12.5b** on adult participation and **A$3.9b** on children’s organised participation outside school. Source: [Australian Sports Commission, 28 April 2023](https://www.sportaus.gov.au/media_centre/news/exercise-worth-%2416.4-billion-a-year-to-aussies)
- `Fact` The same ASC release said the median spend was **A$634 for adults** and **A$650 for children**, with this covering fees paid to an organisation or venue and excluding equipment or travel. Source: same as above.

### Commercial read

- `Inference` Climbers are already accustomed to recurring spend, not just occasional tickets. Membership pricing around `A$30-A$36/week` is a healthy signal for willingness to pay for routine participation.
- `Inference` Event, coaching, youth, and group-booking surfaces are everywhere across gym websites, which is a strong sign that the market supports ancillary spend beyond entry fees.
- `Inference` This is a good environment for future PSF monetisation through sponsorship, featured placements, comp support, and city-scene promotion, especially if PSF becomes a trusted demand aggregator rather than just a content brand.

## City Prioritisation For PSF

### 1. Melbourne

- `Inference` Best first market.
- Reasons:
  dense venue cluster
  strong mix of bouldering-only and mixed gyms
  strong inner-city and near-inner-city concentration
  multiple distinct sub-scenes for content variety
  enough gym count to make weekly utility high

### 2. Sydney

- `Inference` Strong alternative, probably similar or larger in raw demand.
- Tradeoff:
  market feels a bit more geographically spread across inner city, west, south, north, and regional spillover

### 3. Brisbane / SEQ

- `Inference` Excellent expansion market.
- Reasons:
  strong chain presence
  visible state-level event infrastructure
  credible spread from Brisbane into Sunshine Coast, Gold Coast, and Townsville

### 4. Perth

- `Inference` Better than many people would assume.
- CAWA’s current gym page makes Perth look dense and active enough to support meaningful scene coverage.

### 5. Adelaide

- `Inference` Smaller absolute market than Melbourne or Sydney, but its current gym cluster is surprisingly compact and media-friendly.

## What This Means For PSF

- `Inference` The market is clearly large enough to justify a newsletter-first play.
- `Inference` A city-first strategy is still the right one because participation is nationally meaningful but scene activity is locally concentrated.
- `Inference` PSF should optimise for three early monetisation paths:
  sponsor blocks and featured listings
  event promotion and comp amplification
  gym partnership visibility products
- `Inference` The gym count and fragmentation both support the long-term idea of a directory or gym intelligence layer after audience traction.
