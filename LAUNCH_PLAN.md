# Launch Plan

## Launch Moment

Ballina at Youth Nationals in mid-May is the first public launch moment.

This is a launch marketing moment rather than the default first newsletter market.

The working assumption is:

- use Ballina to drive signups and early signal
- use Melbourne as the most likely first newsletter market
- stay open to another market moving quickly if the content and traction justify it

## Goal

Use the event to convert real-world interest into email signups quickly.

## Promotion System

PSF growth should be treated as one connected promotion system with seven lanes:

- launch and field marketing
- comp and giveaway engine
- referral loop
- organic social
- individual ambassadors and sponsor-interest capture
- gym partnerships
- paid social

For launch, the comp and referral engine should be part of the core system, not an optional later add-on.

Gym ambassador ideas should stay later.

Individual ambassador and sponsor-interest capture can exist from day one as a lightweight optional layer, but should not become a heavy program before the main signup loop is proven.

## Launch-Era Priority Order

These lanes are not equally urgent right now.

For launch readiness, the working order is:

1. launch and field marketing
2. comp and giveaway setup
3. signup clarity and referral basics
4. organic social and community proof
5. lightweight ambassador and sponsor-interest capture
6. gym re-share support
7. paid social testing
8. broader ambassador or gym ambassador programs later

This keeps PSF focused on the channels that match the current stage:

- Ballina is a real-world signup moment we can actually execute
- the comp gives people a stronger reason to sign up early
- organic and gym trust are realistic before strong social proof exists
- referral can amplify early signups if the sharing prompt is simple
- optional ambassador and sponsor-interest capture can deepen the list without making signup heavy
- paid social only becomes worth pushing once the signup path, offer, and creative are proven
- gym ambassador structures are better treated as later once we know what actually resonates

## Core Tactics

- print QR cards with a clear signup CTA
- hand them out at the event
- run a comp or giveaway as the list-growth hook
- ask sponsors to support the giveaway
- capture city and interest data at signup

## Core Launch Flywheel

The launch system should work like this:

1. get signups with a useful promise plus a compelling comp
2. turn some of those signups into referrals
3. use early audience and momentum to attract initial sponsors
4. turn sponsor value into better prizes, gear, offers, and visible community perks
5. use those stronger incentives to improve signup, referral, sharing, and social proof

This is the main growth flywheel.

Paid social is only valuable if it feeds this loop rather than buying isolated subscribers.

## Channel Roles

### 1. Launch And Field Marketing

This is the primary launch engine.

It should do the most work in the near term because it matches the first public moment and does not depend on a large existing audience.

Core mechanics:

- QR cards and posters
- on-site CTA and giveaway hook
- simple city-aware signup flow
- clear explanation of what people get and when

### 2. Comp And Giveaway Engine

The comp should be in place before launch.

It is not just a promotional garnish.

It is part of the main conversion mechanic for:

- first signups
- first referrals
- first sponsor conversations

Launch rules:

- keep the mechanic simple
- make the reward story easy to understand
- connect the comp clearly to the value of the newsletter, not as a random unrelated prize
- use sponsor-funded or partner-funded value where possible

Recommended launch shape:

- every signup gets a base entry
- referrals create bonus entries
- extra social actions are optional, not the main mechanic
- prize structure can evolve as sponsor value grows

### 3. Organic Social

Organic should support launch by building legitimacy, repetition, and shareability.

It is not mainly there to be perfectly efficient at conversion.

Its near-term jobs are:

- explain what PSF is
- show real events, gyms, and climbers
- reinforce the giveaway or signup CTA
- create content that gyms and individuals can reshare

### 4. Referral Loop

Referral must be live at launch if possible, because it is part of the main signup economics.

The first version still only needs to do one thing well:

- give each signup a reason to share immediately after joining

Recommended launch-era structure:

- 1 referral = 1 extra giveaway entry
- 3 referrals = small bonus tier
- higher tiers only after the mechanic is proven

If Beehiiv's paid-plan referral system is not ready enough, use the simplest workable fallback and keep the launch moving.

The important thing is the loop, not perfection on day one.

### 5. Individual Ambassadors And Sponsor-Interest Capture

This should exist from the start as an optional interest layer, not as a big public program.

The goal is to identify people and brands who may want deeper involvement later.

Good launch-era captures:

- "Interested in helping promote Pinchy?"
- "Interested in ambassador or street team opportunities?"
- "Brand or sponsor interested in working with us?"

Best placement:

- optional second step after signup or inside the signup flow
- reinforced in the welcome sequence
- captured in Beehiiv custom fields, survey responses, or a simple linked form

The tone can stay intentionally light and exploratory.

We are not promising a large formal program on day one.

We are building a list of people and brands who raise their hand early.

### 6. Gym Partnerships

Gyms matter early, but mainly as trust anchors, event sources, and distribution helpers.

At launch, gym partnerships should emphasize:

- fair inclusion
- event submission and update flow
- permission to share PSF materials
- simple re-share asks

This is more important right now than building a complicated partner tier model.

Gym ambassador or deeper gym program ideas should stay later.

### 7. Paid Social

Paid social should be treated as a test lane, not the main launch bet.

It becomes more useful after:

- the signup page is clear
- the giveaway mechanic works
- the first markets are clearer
- there is enough proof to create believable creative

When it starts, begin with narrow tests rather than a broad national spend.

## What Changes From The Original Promotion Draft

The broader five-lane architecture is directionally right, but for the current stage PSF should refine it in these ways:

- Ballina and offline QR signup are the first real acquisition system, not just one tactic among many
- the comp and referral loop should be treated as launch-critical, not post-launch optimization
- organic and gym support should be prioritized ahead of paid because they build trust and real-world signal
- referral should launch as a simple sharing loop tied to the comp
- ambassador and sponsor-interest capture should begin as a lightweight opt-in layer without promising a heavy program
- gym ambassador ideas should wait until later
- paid social should wait until the message, offer, and landing flow are strong enough to deserve spend

## Launch Assets

The minimum promotion asset set for launch is:

- one clear signup page
- one giveaway hook and rules summary
- one QR flow for Ballina
- one referral explanation and share prompt
- one short organic content pack
- one optional interest-capture step or form for ambassadors and sponsors
- one gym re-share pack

If an asset does not help one of those jobs, it is probably not launch-critical.

## Execution Phases

### Phase 1. Before Ballina

- make the signup path clear
- finalize the giveaway mechanic
- set the launch referral rules and reward copy
- prepare QR assets
- prepare simple organic launch posts
- set up optional ambassador and sponsor-interest capture
- prepare gym outreach and re-share copy
- prepare the welcome and referral prompt

### Phase 2. Ballina Launch Moment

- drive scans and signups
- observe which messaging gets attention
- capture where signups are from
- learn what people ask or hesitate on

### Phase 3. Immediate Follow-On

- push the referral prompt to new signups
- share launch proof on organic
- review which people raise their hand for ambassador or sponsor interest
- follow up with gyms and early supporters
- decide whether Melbourne still leads as the first active newsletter market

### Phase 4. Controlled Expansion

- test city-specific organic content
- test a few individual ambassador activations
- test a few gym partner activations
- test a very small paid social campaign only if the baseline flow is working
- build a clearer sponsor pack once the first traction data exists

## Why This Works

- the audience is already concentrated
- the setting is relevant and high intent
- list growth is immediate and measurable
- sponsors get a concrete activation instead of vague awareness

## What Success Looks Like

- lots of scan-to-signup activity
- a healthy first batch of subscribers
- some evidence that a comp/giveaway attracts attention
- enough signal to decide what city or city mix deserves more focus

## Follow-On

After Ballina, we review:

- what cities showed up
- whether the comp mechanic worked
- whether the sponsor idea felt viable
- whether Melbourne still looks like the best first send market
- what the first newsletter should focus on
- whether referral prompts produced secondary signups
- whether optional ambassador and sponsor-interest capture surfaced useful early people
- whether gyms or individual supporters were willing to reshare
- whether paid social deserves testing yet or should stay parked
