# Launch Plan

## Launch Moment

Ballina at Youth Nationals in mid-May is the first public launch moment.

This is a launch marketing moment rather than the default first newsletter market.

The working assumption is:

- use Ballina to drive signups and early signal
- use Melbourne as the most likely first newsletter market
- stay open to another market moving quickly if the content and traction justify it

## Goal

Use the event to convert real-world interest into email signups quickly.

## Core Tactics

- print QR cards with a clear signup CTA
- hand them out at the event
- run a comp or giveaway as the list-growth hook
- ask sponsors to support the giveaway
- capture city and interest data at signup

## Why This Works

- the audience is already concentrated
- the setting is relevant and high intent
- list growth is immediate and measurable
- sponsors get a concrete activation instead of vague awareness

## What Success Looks Like

- lots of scan-to-signup activity
- a healthy first batch of subscribers
- some evidence that a comp/giveaway attracts attention
- enough signal to decide what city or city mix deserves more focus

## Follow-On

After Ballina, we review:

- what cities showed up
- whether the comp mechanic worked
- whether the sponsor idea felt viable
- whether Melbourne still looks like the best first send market
- what the first newsletter should focus on
