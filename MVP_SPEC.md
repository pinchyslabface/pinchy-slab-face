# MVP Spec

## Working Title

Pinchy Slab Face newsletter MVP.

## Mission

Create the most useful weekly indoor climbing events email for the chosen launch area, then use that audience as the base for future sponsorships, directory products, and related offers.

The bigger ambition is to become the world's most trusted indoor climbing audience and scene platform, but the MVP must stay focused enough to launch and learn.

## The Job To Be Done

Help indoor climbers quickly answer:

- what is on this week
- where is it happening
- what should I not miss

## Target User

Primary user:

- indoor climber who wants local event discovery

Secondary users:

- gyms that want event attendance
- coaches and setters who want visibility
- community organisers who want distribution

## Launch Market

Default launch assumption:

- multi-city from day one

Why:

- the bigger opportunity is global
- traction may come from unexpected cities
- we want city-specific signups from the start
- we can still spotlight whichever city performs best first

Launch tactic:

- enable city-specific signups and "coming soon" availability for other cities
- treat Ballina as the first live launch moment rather than the full market definition

## Core Product

The MVP is not a website.

The MVP is:

- a signup page
- a subscriber list
- a weekly email
- a simple event collection and normalization process

## Product Promise

Simple version:

- never miss what’s on in local climbing

## First Newsletter Format

Recommended issue structure:

1. short intro
2. featured event or sponsor block
3. this week’s events
4. events by day
5. quick gym or climber note
6. submission / reply prompt

Optional recurring content to test later:

- interviews
- climber spotlights
- photo or video of the week
- training tips
- global side-note content

## Signup Flow

1. User lands on the signup page.
2. User enters email.
3. Optional: user chooses home city or region.
4. Optional: user chooses climbing level band.
5. Optional: user chooses primary interest.
6. Optional: user chooses secondary interest.
7. User gets a welcome email.
8. User receives the first weekly issue.

## Welcome Email

The welcome email should:

- confirm they are subscribed
- explain what they will receive
- set expectations on frequency
- invite them to reply with event tips

## Event Collection Flow

Early-stage inputs:

- gym websites
- Instagram posts
- direct gym submissions
- email submissions
- WhatsApp submissions
- manual research
- AI-assisted ingestion helpers for socials, email, and relevant websites

Early-stage processing:

- collect
- normalize
- dedupe
- score confidence
- route uncertain items for review
- tag by city or region
- publish

## Data Fields

Subscriber record:

- email
- home city or region
- climbing level band
- primary interest
- secondary interest
- age band or role if useful
- signup source or QR / UTM metadata

Event record:

- title
- gym name
- city
- state
- date
- event type
- source link
- notes
- source channel
- review status
- confidence score

## Early Growth Channels

- gym reposts
- direct outreach
- posters and QR codes in gyms
- Instagram content
- referrals from climbers
- launch comp / giveaway signups
- event-day QR cards

## First Monetisation Tests

Do not overbuild monetisation. Test simple placements first:

- sponsor block
- featured event
- gym shout-out

## What We Are Avoiding For Now

- full directory build
- login accounts
- mobile app
- complex search
- physical products as a distraction from list growth
- treating source attribution as the core audience segment

We are not avoiding community content or global flavour. We are just treating them as secondary to utility.

## Success Metrics

Good early signals:

- people sign up without being chased too hard
- open rates stay healthy
- subscribers reply with event tips
- gyms are willing to reshare
- the weekly workflow feels repeatable

## Operating Rule

If a task does not help grow the list, improve retention, or validate monetisation, it is not MVP work.
