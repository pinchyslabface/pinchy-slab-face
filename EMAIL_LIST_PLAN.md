# Email List Plan

## Goal

Build a useful indoor climbing email list first, then use that audience to support sponsorships, directory products, and future offers.

## What The List Is For

- weekly event discovery
- gym and climber engagement
- future sponsor inventory
- future segmentation by city, skill, and interest
- long-term scene loyalty and habitual opens

## MVP Audience

Primary audience:

- indoor climbers
- especially people who want to know what is happening this week
- city-specific signups from any market with density

Secondary audience:

- climbing gyms
- coaches
- setters
- event organizers

## MVP Offer

Simple value prop:

- “Never miss what’s on in local climbing.”

Optional variants later:

- city-specific editions
- beginner-focused editions
- competition-focused editions

Planned structure:

- one global brand
- multiple city signups
- "coming soon" cities available from day one
- Melbourne can be the first active city while others sit in waitlist mode
- source attribution is useful, but it is not the main segmentation axis

## Core Flow

1. A climber lands on a simple signup page.
2. They join the email list.
3. They receive a welcome email and the first newsletter.
4. They keep coming back for weekly events and useful community updates.
5. If they are at a live launch event, they scan a QR code and join instantly.

## What We Should Test

- whether people want only events or a broader scene digest
- whether community/people content increases opens and retention
- whether local utility or broader flavour does more of the work
- how much granularity is useful in the city structure

## MVP Shape

The list MVP should be able to answer three questions:

- what is happening
- where is it happening
- why should I care this week

The list should also learn enough about each subscriber to support better matching later, especially:

- home city or region
- climbing level band
- primary interest
- secondary interest
- age band or role when helpful

That means the first issue should feel genuinely useful even if the database behind it is still small.

## What We Should Not Do Yet

- build a full directory
- build user accounts
- build a mobile app
- automate scraping before the process is clear
- add too many content formats too early
- over-focus on source attribution as a subscriber segment

## First Monetisation Tests

- sponsor block in the newsletter
- featured gym or event placement
- local partner shout-outs

## Success Signals

- signups come in without heavy manual chasing
- open rate stays healthy
- people reply with event tips or share the newsletter
- gyms are willing to send events or reshare

## Rule Of Thumb

If it does not grow the list, improve retention, or prove monetisation, it is probably not MVP work.
