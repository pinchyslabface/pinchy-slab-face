# 3D-Printed Climbing Products Business – Project Document (v1)

---

## Project Overview

Build a small-scale, high-margin product business using 3D printing to serve the indoor climbing community.

Initial focus:
- Lightweight, functional accessories
- Non-safety-critical items (low liability)
- Products that are easy to iterate and customise

Primary sales channels:
- Direct-to-consumer (Shopify / Etsy)
- Local climbing gyms (B2B light wholesale)
- Community-driven marketing (Instagram, climber network)

---

## Core Product vs Secondary Features

### 🟢 Core Product (Revenue Drivers)

These are the **main products to focus on first**:

1. **Portable Grip Trainers**
   - Micro hangboards (8–20mm edges)
   - Crimp blocks / pinch blocks
   - Clip-on / carabiner compatible
   - High perceived value, low material cost

2. **Brush Holders & Bag Organisation**
   - Clip-on brush holders (fits chalk bags / backpacks)
   - Modular organisers (brush + tape + nail clippers)
   - Universal fit for different brush sizes

3. **Shoe Deodoriser Inserts**
   - Ventilated cages / capsules
   - Refillable (charcoal, silica, baking soda)
   - Twist-lock or snap-fit designs

👉 These products:
- Solve real problems
- Are easy to prototype
- Have proven demand
- Are safe to sell (non load-bearing)

---

### 🟡 Secondary Products (Expansion / Engagement)

Nice-to-have or later-stage products:

- Gear organisers (carabiner racks, home storage)
- Hangboard accessories (phone mounts, spacers)
- Gym tools (T-nut tools, hold spacers)
- Climbing-themed gifts (keychains, mini holds)
- Travel accessories (shoe inserts, compact kits)

👉 These help:
- Increase AOV (average order value)
- Build brand identity
- Expand catalogue

---

## MVP Scope

### Goal
Launch **3–5 core products** and validate demand quickly.

### Initial Product Set

1. **Micro Hangboard (v1)**
   - 2–3 edge sizes
   - Carabiner hole
   - Optional custom text

2. **Universal Brush Holder (v1)**
   - Fits 5–15mm handles
   - Clip or strap mount
   - Lightweight + durable

3. **Shoe Deodoriser Capsule (v1)**
   - Twist-open design
   - Ventilated shell
   - Refillable

4. *(Optional)* Small organiser clip
   - Holds brush + tape

## Product Design Requirements

### Materials
- PLA (prototype)
- PETG (preferred for durability)
- TPU (optional for flexible elements)

### Print Constraints
- FDM-friendly
- Minimal supports
- Strong layer orientation (critical for grip tools)

### Design Principles
- Lightweight but strong
- Simple assembly (ideally no screws)
- Modular where possible
- Customisable (names, logos)

## Tech Stack Options

### Option 1: No-Code / Fast Launch (Recommended for MVP)

- **Shopify** – storefront
- **Canva** – branding/assets
- **Google Forms + Sheets** – custom order intake
- **Instagram / TikTok** – marketing

Pros:
- Fast to launch
- Low technical overhead

Cons:
- Limited flexibility

---

### Option 2: Custom Stack (Later Stage)

- Frontend: React / Next.js
- Backend: Node.js or headless CMS (e.g. Strapi)
- Database: PostgreSQL

Use case:
- Scaling customisation
- Managing product variants
- Automating workflows

## User Flows / Operations

### 1. Customer Purchase Flow
1. User visits store
2. Selects product
3. Adds customisation (optional)
4. Purchases
5. Order stored

---

### 2. Production Flow
1. Review daily orders
2. Batch prints by product type
3. Post-process (clean, assemble)
4. Package
5. Ship

---

### 3. Gym Sales Flow (Manual MVP)
1. Approach gym
2. Show samples
3. Offer wholesale pricing
4. Take bulk order
5. Print + deliver

## Costs

### Startup Costs
- 3D printer (existing or ~$1k–2k)

### Ongoing Costs
- Filament
- Shipping
- Payment fees

---

## Risks & Constraints

### ⚠️ Product Risks
- Weak prints (layer failure)
- Poor ergonomics
- Competition from existing products

### ⚠️ Business Risks
- Low demand without brand
- Copycat products
- Pricing pressure

### ⚠️ Legal / Safety
DO NOT SELL:
- Load-bearing gear (carabiners, anchors)
- Safety-critical climbing equipment

---

## Key Insights

- Climbers value **small, useful, well-designed tools**
- Portability and aesthetics matter
- Customisation is a strong differentiator
- Community trust is important → leverage local gyms

---

## Recommended Approach

### Phase 1: Validate Fast
- Design 2–3 products
- Print prototypes
- Test with local climbers
- Iterate quickly

### Phase 2: Soft Launch
- Launch Etsy or Shopify
- Post in climbing communities
- Partner with 1–2 gyms

### Phase 3: Expand
- Add product variations
- Introduce bundles
- Build brand identity
