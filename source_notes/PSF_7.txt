# Project Specification: "CRAG & CANVAS" Streetwear Collection

## 1. Project Overview
**Objective:** Launch a premium, independent streetwear brand specifically targeting the bouldering and sport climbing subcultures. 
**Core Value Proposition:** Bridging the gap between technical climbing lingo and quirky, vintage-inspired streetwear aesthetics. The brand avoids "earnest" outdoor tropes in favor of irony, randomness, and "insider" subculture humor.

---

## 2. Product Strategy: Signal vs. Noise

### Core Product (The Value)
* **The "Vintage Ad" Series:** High-concept graphic tees mimicking 1950s–1980s print advertisements for fictional climbing products.
* **Subculture Lingo Integration:** Creative use of "Send," "Beta," "Dyno," "Crimp," and "Projecting" without being derivative.
* **Premium Streetwear Fit:** Heavyweight cotton, boxy/oversized cuts (standard for modern streetwear) rather than slim-fit technical "activewear."

### Supporting Features (Growth & Engagement)
* **Sticker Packs:** High-margin accessories using the same quirky motifs (e.g., the "Monocled Hand" or "Climbing Yeti").
* **Community "Beta" Cards:** Small postcards included in orders with nonsensical/funny "beta" for famous world landmarks.
* **Limited "Drops":** Scarcity-based release model to build brand hype and minimize unsold inventory.

---

## 3. MVP Scope (The "First Ascent" Collection)
To minimize initial overhead, the launch will focus on five "Hero" designs:

1.  **Grip-Rite Chalk:** Vintage anatomical hand illustration with a monocle.
2.  **Boulder-Buster Yeti:** 1950s-style "tough-guy" ad featuring a bewildered Yeti.
3.  **The "Send-It" Unicorn:** A surrealist, high-energy graphic for the "randomness" factor.
4.  **Crimper Cream:** A parody product label design focused on "Fingers of Steel."
5.  **Beta Biscuits:** A retro tin-can design playing on the "feeding beta" trope.

---

## 4. Production & Tech Stack Options

### Option A: Print-on-Demand (Low Risk)
* **Platform:** Printful or Printify integrated with **Shopify**.
* **Pros:** Zero inventory cost; automated fulfillment.
* **Cons:** Lower margins; less control over garment quality and custom labeling.

### Option B: Small Batch Local Production (High Quality - Recommended)
* **Platform:** **Shopify** (Front-end) + **Klaviyo** (Email Marketing).
* **Production:** Local screen-printing on high-quality blanks (e.g., LA Apparel, Rue Porter).
* **Pros:** Superior print quality (essential for vintage aesthetics); custom neck labels; higher brand "clout."

---

## 5. Data Model (Product Catalog Structure)

| Entity | Attributes |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Product** | ID, Title, Design_Series (e.g., Vintage Ad), Base_Color, Price |
| **Variant** | SKU, Size (S-XXL), Stock_Level |
| **Graphic Asset** | Filename, Ink_Colors (Pantone), Placement (Chest/Back/Sleeve) |
| **Lingo_Tag** | Keyword (e.g., "Dyno", "Pumped") for SEO and Filtering |

---

## 6. Operations & Workflow

1.  **Design Phase:** Finalize vector art -> Apply "Aged/Distressed" textures -> Select Pantone colors.
2.  **Sampling:** Order test prints to ensure fine lines on "Vintage Ad" text remain legible.
3.  **Content Capture:** "Street/Crag" lookbook shoot (blurry, lo-fi aesthetic to match the brand).
4.  **Launch:** Email blast to "Early Access" list -> Social media drop.
5.  **Fulfillment:** Orders synced from Shopify to shipping station (ShipStation).

---

## 7. Risks & Constraints

* **Niche Saturation:** The climbing apparel market is growing; the "quirky" angle must remain authentic to avoid being "cringe."
* **Technical Legibility:** Vintage fonts can be thin. Must ensure screen-printing mesh is high enough to capture detail.
* **Sizing Expectations:** Streetwear buyers prefer specific fits. Clear size charts comparing "Street Fit" vs. "Climbing Fit" are mandatory.

---

## 8. Recommended Approach

**Start with Option B (Small Batch).** Streetwear thrives on the *feel* and weight of the garment. For an independent designer brand, a standard POD tee will lack the premium edge required for high-ticket pricing. Produce **30–50 units per design** on premium heavyweight blanks. Focus marketing heavily on **Instagram and TikTok**, using "Wait for the Drop" countdowns to build a sense of exclusivity and community.
