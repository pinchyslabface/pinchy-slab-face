# Sponsor Discovery Playbook

## Goal

Build a near-complete sponsor database for an Australian climbing newsletter by starting from real climbing events and community partners, then expanding outward into the brands that repeatedly support the scene.

## Best discovery approach

The most effective workflow is:

1. start host-first, not brand-first
2. log every confirmed event from the last 2 to 3 years
3. extract every sponsor named on event pages, recap pages, posters, and socials
4. normalize sponsors into one master list
5. score sponsors by overlap, scene fit, and likely newsletter spend

This produces much better coverage than generic searching for "climbing sponsor" because most real sponsor evidence lives on:

- gym event pages
- state body event pages
- event recap posts
- sponsor thank-you captions
- partner/supporter pages
- membership perk pages
- event posters with logo blocks

## Priority source stack

### Tier 1. Official event pages

These are the cleanest evidence sources.

Search and log:

- state titles
- youth titles
- open titles
- gym comps
- boulder ladders
- social comp nights
- fundraiser comps

Useful query patterns:

- `site:domain.com climbing event sponsor`
- `site:domain.com boulder comp sponsor`
- `site:domain.com "special thanks" climbing`
- `site:domain.com "partners and sponsors"`

### Tier 2. Host partner pages

These surface likely sponsor and partner prospects even when an event page does not list sponsors.

Examples:

- state association `partners` / `supporters` pages
- gym `community partners` pages
- club `member perks` pages

These are especially useful for identifying:

- gyms willing to pay to reach climbers
- climbing-adjacent retailers
- service providers
- brands already spending on the community

### Tier 3. Posters and recap graphics

Many climbing comps list the most useful sponsor names only on posters or logo tiles.

Best process:

1. capture the poster image or recap graphic
2. manually transcribe sponsor logos for the first pass
3. later batch OCR logo/poster assets once the naming rules are stable

### Tier 4. Social announcements

When official pages are thin, the missing data is often in:

- Instagram captions
- Facebook event pages
- finals recap posts
- "thanks to our sponsors" stories or carousels

This should be the main second-wave research layer after official sites.

## Exhaustive search matrix

Run the same search matrix for every major operator, venue, and state body.

### Host queries

- `"[host name]" climbing competition`
- `"[host name]" boulder comp`
- `"[host name]" sponsor`
- `"[host name]" event partners`
- `site:[host domain] events`
- `site:[host domain] competition`

### State / city queries

- `Victoria climbing comp sponsor`
- `Queensland bouldering comp sponsors`
- `Sydney climbing gym competition sponsor`
- `Melbourne bouldering comp partners`

### Sponsor-backfill queries

Once one sponsor is found, search:

- `"[brand]" climbing comp Australia`
- `"[brand]" bouldering sponsor`
- `"[brand]" "sport climbing"`

This is how we find second and third appearances for overlap scoring.

## How to score prospects

For newsletter sponsorship, event appearance alone is not enough. Score each prospect across three axes:

### 1. Overlap

How often do they appear across:

- multiple events
- multiple hosts
- multiple states

### 2. Scene fit

How close are they to climbing?

- highest fit: gear brands, gyms, training brands, resolers, holds, physios, coaching
- medium fit: outdoor retailers, adventure apparel, energy/food, event photographers
- lower fit: hotels, breweries, tourism, local hospitality

### 3. Likely spend type

What are they likely to buy?

- paid ad placement
- giveaway / prize support
- affiliate offer
- editorial partnership
- event calendar or series sponsorship

## Prospect buckets

These should all live in the final database:

- climbing gear brands
- outdoor retailers
- gyms and operators
- hold / wall / routesetting suppliers
- coaching and physio services
- photography / media services
- food and beverage brands already active at comps
- travel / hotel / tourism partners around destination events
- distributors who can unlock several brands at once
- community organizations with amplification or intro value

## What to do next

### Next most valuable expansion

1. mine every state-body event page from 2023 to 2025
2. mine every major gym operator event page from the same period
3. capture sponsor logos from posters and Instagram recaps
4. backfill direct contact paths for the A-tier prospects

### Highest-yield targets to prioritize first

- repeat commercial sponsors
- climbing-native service providers
- retailers already supporting comps
- gym operators with broad audience overlap

Those are the most plausible early newsletter buyers.
