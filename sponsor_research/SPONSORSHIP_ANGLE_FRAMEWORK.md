# Sponsorship Angle Framework

This file separates the sponsor ask into the main motions PSF is likely to run.

The key idea is that these are not separate sponsor universes.

There will be heavy overlap between:

- launch giveaway product
- street team or sponsored climber product support
- PSF merch or package support
- long-term paid sponsorship

The best foundational sponsors are the ones that can help across more than one of these.

## 1. Launch Giveaway Product

Best fit:

- chalk
- skin care
- recovery products
- training accessories
- retailer-provided prize bundles

Strong current examples:

- `Rhino Skin Solutions`
- `Climbskin`
- `Friction Labs`
- `Chalk Cartel`
- `Reset`
- `Awesome Woodys`
- `Crux Gear`
- `Climbing Anchors`

Why this matters:

- easiest early ask
- strong perceived value for climbers
- easiest path to proving sponsor appetite before audience scale is fully established

## 2. Street Team / Sponsored Climber Product Support

Best fit:

- consumables for repeat use
- training items
- selected shoe support
- selected retailer or distributor support

Strong current examples:

- `Scarpa Australia`
- `La Sportiva Australia`
- `Unparallel`
- `Evolv`
- `Tenaya`
- `Rhino Skin Solutions`
- `Climbskin`
- `Friction Labs`
- `Chalk Cartel`
- `Reset`
- `Awesome Woodys`
- `Crux Gear`
- `Lattice Training`

Why this matters:

- strong story around supporting youth and emerging talent
- especially relevant if PSF gets meaningful buy-in around the `Youth National Titles in Ballina`
- high social amplification potential because youth climbers and emerging climbers are often highly active online

## 3. PSF Merch / Package Support

Best fit:

- apparel partners
- retailer partners
- broader outdoor brands
- sponsors willing to help fund shipping or physical package economics

Strong current examples:

- `Paddy Pallin`
- `K2 Base Camp`
- `Climbing Anchors`
- `Happy Biner`
- `Mammut`

Why this matters:

- not every sponsor has to supply branded product directly
- some may help through:
  - voucher support
  - subsidized product access
  - apparel collaboration
  - freight or package budget support

## 4. Long-Term Paid Sponsors

Best fit:

- brands with repeat climbing-scene spend
- brands already comfortable with media or event activation
- category leaders with national relevance

Strong current examples:

- `La Sportiva Australia`
- `Scarpa Australia`
- `Black Diamond`
- `Lattice Training`
- `Rhino Skin Solutions`
- `Climbing Anchors`
- `Paddy Pallin`

Why this matters:

- this is the most important long-term lane
- but it probably comes after early proof through giveaways, youth support, and visible scene traction

## Best Foundational Sponsor Types

These are the most valuable because they can stretch across multiple angles:

### 1. Foundational Multi-Angle Brands

- `La Sportiva Australia`
- `Scarpa Australia`
- `Black Diamond`
- `Lattice Training`

They can plausibly support:

- launch credibility
- youth-athlete association
- selective product support
- longer-term paid partnership

### 2. Product Seed Partners

- `Rhino Skin Solutions`
- `Climbskin`
- `Friction Labs`
- `Chalk Cartel`
- `Reset`
- `Awesome Woodys`
- `Crux Gear`
- `Redpoint Climbing`

They are ideal for:

- packs
- giveaways
- social posting
- youth and street-team seeding

### 3. Retail / Bundle Partners

- `Climbing Anchors`
- `Paddy Pallin`
- `K2 Base Camp`
- `Happy Biner`
- `The Climbing Shop`
- `Pinnacle Sports`

They can help with:

- mixed product bundles
- vouchers
- apparel or accessory access
- package economics
- brand introductions

## Recommendation

The best sponsorship logic for PSF is not:

- find one sponsor for one thing

It is:

- find a small number of foundational sponsors that can participate across multiple angles

In practice:

- use consumables and training brands for easy early product support
- use retailers and distributors to help build better mixed packages
- use shoe and premium brands as long-term upside targets
- tie the youth story directly to Ballina and the next generation of highly social climbers
