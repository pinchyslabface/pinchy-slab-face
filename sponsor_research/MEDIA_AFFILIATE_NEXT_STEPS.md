# Media And Affiliate Next Steps

## Why this matters

The sponsor research is now strong on:

- event sponsors
- brands
- distributors
- retailers
- gym pro shops
- shoe-repair networks

The next high-value layer is:

- climbing media
- affiliate-capable retailers
- advertiser infrastructure that already sells to climbing brands

Important priority note:

Affiliate should stay secondary.

It is a useful monetisation option to annotate, but it should not distract from the main research goal:

- building a strong map of brands
- distributor and wholesaler routes
- retailer and gym stock pathways
- historical sponsor and advertiser behaviour

This matters because it helps answer two important questions:

1. who already spends money to reach climbers?
2. which monetisation models are already proven in this category?

## Most important takeaway from the external notes

Do not think of sponsorship only as:

- direct logo buys
- comp prize support
- retailer feature spots

Also think in terms of:

- media buyers
- affiliate offers
- demo-tour amplification
- B2B setter and gym-industry audiences

## Best next research moves

### 1. Audit the climbing media layer

Priority targets:

- `Vertical Life`
- `theCrag`
- `The Hangout Podcast`
- `Sport Climbing Australia`

What to capture:

- whether they sell ads, sponsored content, partnerships, or directory placements
- which climbing or outdoor advertisers appear repeatedly
- whether they publish media kits, rate cards, sponsor pages, or partner pages
- which brands seem to buy editorial attention versus just event support

This is the best next step from the notes.

It will show which brands are already trained to buy climbing media.

### 2. Audit affiliate-capable retailer paths

Priority targets:

- `Wild Earth`
- `Paddy Pallin`
- `Climbing Anchors`
- `Mountain Equipment`

Specific question:

- which of these already have affiliate or referral infrastructure?

The external notes specifically call out:

- `Commission Factory`

That means we should run a focused pass on:

- retailer affiliate networks
- coupon/referral structures
- stockist pages that could support gear recommendations

But keep this pass intentionally light.

The purpose is only to note whether the path exists, not to over-optimise affiliate strategy at this stage.

### 3. Create a "setting audience" sponsor bucket

Because the product is a weekly "what's on" and "fresh sets" newsletter, a unique sponsor lane exists:

- hold brands
- routesetting suppliers
- gym software
- setter tools

Priority research bucket:

- `Stax`
- `Aura Climbing`
- `Uncarved Block`
- `Walltopia`
- `Vertical-Life App`
- `ROLLER`

The point is not just to sell to climbers.

It is also to sell to the infrastructure around weekly setting activity.

### 4. Add a practical monetisation lens to the current sponsor CRM

The current CRM is good at mapping entities.

The next thing it needs is a clearer separation between:

- likely paid sponsor
- likely affiliate partner
- likely giveaway partner
- likely intro path only

That will prevent high-noise prospecting later.

## What we should do next in this thread

1. research `Vertical Life` and other climbing media surfaces
2. lightly annotate affiliate routes for `Wild Earth`, `Paddy Pallin`, and any other obvious retailer path
3. identify repeated advertisers or partner brands from those channels
4. feed those brands back into `sponsors_master.csv`

## Working thesis

The strongest monetisation path is probably a blend of:

- direct sponsorship from climbing-native brands and specialist retailers
- affiliate revenue from retailer gear recommendations
- later B2B sponsorship from setting and gym-industry suppliers

But the research priority should remain:

1. market map first
2. sponsor and advertiser behaviour second
3. affiliate as a supporting note, not a dominant lane
