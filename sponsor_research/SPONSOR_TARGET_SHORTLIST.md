# Sponsor Target Shortlist

## Best Current Targets

The shortlist is now intentionally biased toward `consumer-facing` and `community-service` sponsors first.

Retailers and distributors still matter a lot, but mostly as:

- direct retail sponsors
- intro paths to brands
- infrastructure nodes in the market map

That means we are no longer treating every important climbing-economy company as the same kind of prospect.

## Current PSF Sponsorship Motions

The current sponsor logic is not just about one generic newsletter sponsorship ask.

We now have four overlapping motions:

- launch giveaway product
- street team or sponsored climber product support
- PSF merch or package support
- long-term paid sponsorship

The most valuable targets are the `foundational` ones that can stretch across more than one of these motions.

This matters because:

- giveaway and product support are likely the easiest early asks
- youth and street-team support creates a stronger social and cultural story than a pure ad buy
- package and merch support may come from retailers or broader outdoor partners even when they are not the best long-term headline sponsors
- long-term paid sponsors remain the highest-value objective once traction is proven

The `2026 National Youth Championships` in `Ballina, NSW` strengthen this logic even if a public sponsor wall is not yet visible:

- the event is a concentrated youth and pathway audience
- it supports the “back the next generation” story
- it increases the relevance of street-team and youth-athlete product seeding
- it gives premium and youth-facing brands a stronger reason to participate early

## Tier C: Keep In The Map, Lower Immediate Sponsor Priority

- local food and beverage brands
- tourism and hotel partners
- local event-only hospitality supporters
- government and community campaign funders

## Recommended Next Step

The best immediate move is to keep research and sponsorship motions aligned:

1. use `consumer-facing` and `community-service` brands as the default top-of-list sponsor pool
2. prioritize brands that can help across multiple motions, not just one
3. use retailers and distributors as contact paths, validators, and mixed-bundle partners
4. keep Ballina and the youth-pathway story central when evaluating early product-support relevance
5. keep B2B suppliers in the map without letting them crowd the first-wave sponsor strategy

That gives us a cleaner sponsor thesis while still preserving the full climbing-commerce map underneath it.
