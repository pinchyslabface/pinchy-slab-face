# Sponsor Target Shortlist

## Best Current Targets

The shortlist is now intentionally biased toward `consumer-facing` and `community-service` sponsors first.

Retailers and distributors still matter a lot, but mostly as:

- direct retail sponsors
- intro paths to brands
- infrastructure nodes in the market map

That means we are no longer treating every important climbing-economy company as the same kind of prospect.

## Tier A: First-Wave Climbers-Facing Targets

- `La Sportiva Australia`
  Strongest current commercial activation signal with a six-stop 2026 gym tour across NSW and VIC.
- `Scarpa Australia`
  Repeated demo and activation signal with high-fit shoe category relevance.
- `Sticky Rubber Resoles`
  Best service-lane prospect with strong brand-authorisation and store-drop-off network.
- `Climbing Resoles`
  Strong national service prospect with agent/partner model and gym/retailer integration.
- `Black Diamond`
  High-fit global climbing brand with repeat sponsor evidence and broad retail presence.
- `Climbit Physio`
  Strong editorial fit for injury-prevention, performance, and recovery content.
- `Awesome Woodys`
  Very strong training-product fit and Australian climbing-native identity.
- `Climbskin`
  High-utility hand-care category with obvious recurring relevance for committed climbers.
- `Unparallel`
  Strong performance shoe fit with a clear Australian distributor path.
- `Ocun`
  Climbing-native gear brand with clear Australian route and strong product-category relevance.
- `Evolv`
  Performance shoe category with clear retail visibility in-market.
- `Tenaya`
  High-fit shoe brand for indoor-performance and project-driven editorial.
- `Beastmaker`
  Training-product fit is very strong for a gym-heavy climbing audience.
- `Crux Gear`
  Homegrown training brand with obvious editorial and giveaway potential.
- `Redpoint Climbing`
  Australian climbing brand with direct consumer relevance and stockist footprint.

## Tier C: Keep In The Map, Lower Immediate Sponsor Priority

- local food and beverage brands
- tourism and hotel partners
- local event-only hospitality supporters
- government and community campaign funders
- B2B wall, hold, and gym-ops suppliers unless the audience expands toward setters or operators

These are still useful to map, but are lower priority for newsletter revenue unless tied to a local event package.

## Recommended Next Step

The best immediate move is to keep research and prioritisation aligned:

1. use `consumer-facing` and `community-service` brands as the default top-of-list sponsor pool
2. use retailers and distributors as contact paths, validators, and secondary sponsor options
3. keep B2B suppliers in the map without letting them crowd the first-wave sponsor strategy

That gives us a cleaner sponsor thesis while still preserving the full climbing-commerce map underneath it.
