# Sponsor Filtered Views

This file turns the tag layer into practical sponsor slices we can use quickly.

These filtered views are most useful when paired with the current PSF sponsorship motions:

- launch giveaway product
- street team or sponsored climber product support
- PSF merch or package support
- long-term paid sponsorship

The same sponsor may appear in more than one useful motion.

## 1. Endemic + Proven Scene Spenders

These are the clearest climbing-native brands or retailers that already show real scene spend.

- `La Sportiva Australia`
- `Scarpa Australia`
- `Black Diamond`
- `Climbing Anchors`
- `Happy Biner`

This is the cleanest set for:

- long-term paid sponsorship
- demo nights
- launch giveaways
- city or gym-specific activations
- youth-pathway and Ballina-aligned credibility

## 2. Adjacent + Proven Scene Spenders

These are not strictly endemic climbing brands, but they have evidence of targeting climbers already.

- `Paddy Pallin`
- `K2 Base Camp`
- `Sea Legs Brewing Co.`
- `Climbit Physio`
- `Rocktape`
- `Rock Therapy BNE`
- `Clif Bar Australia`
- `Nalgene Australia`
- `Tenzing`

This set is especially useful for:

- launch partner asks
- street team support
- recovery and nutrition bundles
- local event or youth-facing support
- lower-friction early sponsor asks before audience scale is fully proven

## 3. Media-Familiar Adjacent Brands

These brands and retailers look useful later because they already appear in climbing-media contexts.

- `Arc'teryx`
- `Wild Earth`
- `Mountain Equipment`
- `Bogong Equipment`
- `Summit Gear`
- `Mammut`
- `Patagonia`
- `The North Face`

These are better for:

- longer-term paid sponsor conversations
- premium brand association
- later-stage outreach once the audience is proven
- possible merch or package-support partners where premium brand halo matters

## 4. Local Event-Only Adjacent Brands

These should stay in the map, but they are not first-wave newsletter targets.

- `Browne's Dairy`
- `Szent`
- `Pat & Stick's`
- `The Biltong Box Mandurah`
- `Atrium Mandurah Hotel`
- `Mandurah Dolphin Cruises`
- `Little Sammys`

These are mainly useful for:

- local comp packaging
- launch event support
- low-expectation one-off partnerships
- geo-specific hospitality or travel support

## 5. Industry-Only Map Nodes

These matter commercially, but mostly as infrastructure or access routes.

- `Stax Climbing`
- `ALS Trade`
- `Friction Addiction`
- `Spelean`
- `Southern Cross Equipment`

These are best treated as:

- map nodes
- contact paths
- possible B2B opportunities
- low-priority direct newsletter targets unless the audience broadens
- possible bundle builders when a distributor unlocks multiple useful product lines

## Recommended Use

- For `paid sponsor` targeting: start with `Endemic + Proven Scene Spenders`, then move to `Media-Familiar Adjacent Brands`.
- For `launch giveaway product`: use `Endemic + Proven Scene Spenders` plus the best of `Adjacent + Proven Scene Spenders`.
- For `street team / sponsored climber product`: prioritize chalk, skin care, training items, and retailer/distributor partners with visible consumables access.
- For `PSF merch / package support`: prioritize specialist retailers, outdoor retailers, and adjacent premium brands that can help with bundles, apparel access, or physical package economics.
- For `local event` support: use `Local Event-Only Adjacent Brands` sparingly and only when the package is geo-specific.
