# Media Surface Notes

## What this pass is for

This pass is not trying to prove paid ad buys with certainty from every article mention.

It is trying to identify:

- climbing media or platform surfaces that clearly sell access to climbers
- recurring brand presence on those surfaces
- evidence that certain brands are already comfortable appearing in climbing-media contexts

## Current takeaways

### theCrag

Strong signal.

Public evidence shows:

- `theCrag` explicitly offers `Business Sponsorship`
- it sells reach on worldwide, country-specific, or regional levels
- it names `gear manufacturers` as a target partner category
- it also supports gyms, route setters, advocacy groups, and local businesses

This makes `theCrag` important not as a sponsor target first, but as proof that:

- climbing platforms can sell targeted audience access
- B2B and geo-specific sponsorship are already normal in this category

### Vertical Life

Strong signal.

Public evidence shows repeated editorial gear presence from brands like:

- `La Sportiva`
- `Black Diamond`
- `Petzl`
- `Arc'teryx`
- `C.A.M.P.`
- `Mammut`
- `Edelrid`

There is also a much stronger commercial signal now:

- `Vertical Life` has a public `Advertise with us` page
- the page lists a named commercial contact
- the page shows a visible brand wall that includes climbing and outdoor companies such as `Wild Earth`, `Arc'teryx`, `Mountain Equipment`, `theCrag`, `Black Diamond`, `Bogong Equipment`, `Summit Gear`, `Mammut`, `Climbing Anchors`, `Scarpa`, `Patagonia`, `Petzl`, and others
- `Vertical Life` also has a public `Stockists` page that distributes the magazine through specialist retailers and gyms including `Paddy Pallin`, `Climbing Anchors`, `Wild Earth`, `Pinnacle Sports`, `Wall Toys`, `Bogong Equipment`, `Sydney Indoor Climbing Gym`, `Pulse Climbing`, `Beyond Bouldering`, and others

Important caveat:

This does not automatically prove each article was paid.

But it does show:

- these brands repeatedly appear in a climbing-media environment
- Vertical Life has a gear and brand-coverage surface that is commercially relevant
- Vertical Life also has a real retail and gym distribution footprint, not just an editorial website
- the same premium gear cluster keeps showing up
- Vertical Life very likely sits inside an existing climbing/outdoor advertiser network

### The Hangout Australian Climbing Podcast

Emerging signal.

Public evidence shows:

- an active Australian climbing podcast
- references to a Substack and guest line-up
- recurring guest and community-story format
- at least one public episode listing tied to a `The North Face` athlete guest

Important caveat:

This is not yet enough to call it a proven sponsor seller.

But it is enough to treat it as:

- a media/community surface worth monitoring
- a possible future sponsor or partner context source

### What this means for sponsor research

The media pass is useful because it helps identify:

- brands already trained to show up in climbing-specific media
- the premium cluster of likely newsletter-fit advertisers
- the difference between one-off event sponsors and category-level media buyers

## Best next research moves

1. Cross-check the `Vertical Life` brand wall against our existing sponsor and gear ecosystem map.
2. Add a light `media_presence_signal` note to the strongest overlapping brands in the sponsor CRM if that overlap becomes meaningful.
3. Run a similar pass on `The Hangout Podcast` and any major Australian climbing creator or guide surface that visibly supports partners.
4. Keep affiliate-related notes light unless they directly improve the brand/distributor/retailer map.
